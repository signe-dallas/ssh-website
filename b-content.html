Practical Next Steps

Student success is a shared responsibility--but without the resources to sustain impactful programs, even the most innovative student affairs initiatives can struggle to thrive.

As institutions continue to evolve in response to student needs, financial literacy among student affairs professionals is no longer a nice-to-have--it's a necessity.

When student affairs leaders understand how budget cycles work, how to advocate for funding using data, and how to align their programs with institutional priorities, they are better positioned to:

  • Secure and sustain resources for key initiatives
  • Demonstrate impact using financial and student success data
  • Collaborate effectively with chief financial officers and budget officers while contributing meaningfully to integrated planning and strategy conversations

To strengthen financial fluency, institutions can start with practical, manageable actions:

  1. Offer cross-functional training opportunities
  2. Create shared planning conversations
  3. Use data to connect programs with institutional priorities
