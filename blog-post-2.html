Elevating Financial Acumen in Student Affairs to Strengthen Student Success

[Month XX, XXXX]   |   By Dr. Takeem Dean, Frank Dorsey, II and Dr. Kelli Rainey

Read time: 5 minutes

Student affairs professionals shape transformative student experiences, but without financial fluency, even the most impactful programs can fall short.

Financial acumen is not just professional development. It is a strategic necessity for advancing student success.

Quick Takeaways

  • Financial literacy enables student affairs leaders to secure, sustain, and scale programs.
  • Strong financial knowledge improves collaboration with finance and budget leaders.
  • Cross-functional alignment leads to more effective, student-centered strategies.
  • The NACUBO Student Success Hub provides tools to connect finance with student success outcomes.

Why Financial Acumen Matters for Student Affairs

Student success is a shared responsibility, but sustaining meaningful programs requires more than vision. It requires resources, planning, and financial strategy.

Student affairs divisions play a critical role in shaping the student experience, from leadership development and wellness to engagement and belonging. These efforts directly influence persistence and success.

However, many professionals in student-facing roles have not received formal training in:

  • Budgeting
  • Forecasting
  • Financial planning

This creates a disconnect between program impact and financial decision-making.

When student affairs leaders understand institutional finance, they are better positioned to:

  • Secure and sustain resources for key initiatives
  • Demonstrate impact using financial and student success data
  • Collaborate effectively with chief business and budget officers
  • Contribute to institutional planning and strategy

Breaking Silos and Building Financial Capacity

Closing the gap between student affairs and finance requires intentional effort. Institutions must create opportunities for collaboration, shared learning, and transparency.

Effective approaches include:

  • Joint training sessions for student affairs and finance staff
  • Mentorship or peer-learning cohorts focused on budget literacy
  • Cross-functional planning teams aligned to student success goals
  • Shared dashboards that integrate financial and student outcome data

When student affairs leaders can confidently engage in financial conversations, they become stronger stewards of institutional resources and more effective advocates for students.

How the NACUBO Student Success Hub Supports This Work

For professionals looking to strengthen their financial fluency, the NACUBO Student Success Hub offers practical tools and resources designed to bridge student success and financial strategy.

The Hub includes:

  • Interactive toolkits linking student success initiatives to financial practices
  • Case studies demonstrating alignment between budgeting and student outcomes
  • Guides for working with finance leaders and using data to advocate for resources
  • Professional development materials that build shared understanding across departments

Whether you are new to budget planning or working to align your division's impact with institutional metrics, the Hub provides actionable, real-world guidance.

What This Means for Student Affairs Leaders

Elevating financial acumen is not an add-on. It is a core leadership competency.

Three key shifts matter most:

1. Financial Acumen Is a Strategic Asset

Financial literacy goes beyond understanding spreadsheets. It enables leaders to:

  • Align programs with institutional priorities
  • Communicate value in terms decision-makers understand
  • Advocate effectively for resources

2. Collaboration with Finance Leaders Is Essential

Strong partnerships with chief business officers and budget teams are critical.

When student affairs and finance work together, institutions can:

  • Develop more sustainable strategies
  • Align funding with student outcomes
  • Make more informed, data-driven decisions

3. Intentional Learning Drives Greater Impact

Building financial fluency does not happen overnight, but steady, intentional learning makes a difference.

Consider:

  • Participating in training opportunities
  • Seeking mentorship or peer learning
  • Engaging in cross-divisional conversations

These steps expand both individual leadership capacity and institutional impact.

What to Do Next

Investing in financial acumen strengthens not only student affairs professionals but the students they serve.

Explore the NACUBO Student Success Hub to:

  • Build your financial fluency
  • Strengthen cross-campus collaboration
  • Align your work with institutional strategy

Because when student affairs leaders are financially empowered, student success is strengthened.
